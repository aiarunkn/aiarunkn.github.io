I grew up in Astana — a city willed into existence on the Kazakh steppe, where the skyline is all ambition and glass. That particular energy — building the new, fast, from scratch — has felt familiar to me for as long as I can remember.

I study at Minerva University, a school without a campus. We rotate through seven cities — San Francisco, Seoul, Hyderabad, Berlin, Buenos Aires, London, Taipei — carrying our work with us. Mine is building AI agents: systems that don't just process, but reason, adapt, and act.

Kenges started with a question: why does AI always feel foreign to Central Asia? A business advisor that actually understands the region — its context, its culture, its language of doing business — felt more necessary than another generic chatbot.

Also drawn to: urban futures, nomadic philosophy, the aesthetics of new cities.
