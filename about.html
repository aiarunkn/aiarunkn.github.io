01 One take

I learned not to need a second take.

As a kid, I sang on stages. You stop caring about people's opinions pretty fast when you're under the lights — and you learn that the take you give is the take they keep.

Directors couldn't burn time reshooting me, so I learned to land it the first time. That stuck. I'm extremely adaptable and a fast study — I'd rather understand a thing well enough to do it once, cleanly, than circle it forever.

02 What it cost

I know exactly what an opportunity costs — so I don't waste them.

We sold my horse to cover my first year at university. Even with 96% off tuition and board, the rest had to come from somewhere. I don't bring it up for sympathy. I bring it up because most people never have to put a number on their ambition, and I did early.

Now, in my second year, I manage a team of sales and developers. I freelanced my way in. The throughline is the same: show up, read the terrain, ship.
